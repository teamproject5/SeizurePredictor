FAQ

How often is the data updated?
The Seizure Predictor is a service which analyzes the latest given data from the EEG device every minute.


How do I interpret the Seizure Predictor status on the Prediction page?
There are three possible results the Seizure Predictor will present:

1. Status set to "Ok" and background is green - This icon indicates that the measured brain activity is normal.
2. Status set to "Warning" and background is yellow - This icon indicates that the mesaured brain activity is more sporadic and a seizure may come.
3. Status set to "Seizure!" and background is red - This icon indicates that a seizure is about to come if it has not already. TAKE COVER AND PUT YOURSELF IN A SAFE POSITION!


How can I create an account?
This service is strictly meant for patients with epilepsy or other seizure-causing disorders. All accounts are created by doctors or the Seizure Predictor IT team. Please contact your doctor or click here to call us directly for more information.


How accurate is the prediction result?
Based on extensive lab test cases, the Seizure Predictor will accurately predict a seizure every 6 out of 8 predictions. The Seizure Predictor is designed to give false positives, such that the Seizure Predictor will indicate that a seizure will occur when in fact it does not.


Is a mobile application of the Seizure Predictor available?
Currently, there is not a mobile appiication available. But we expect this application will be available in the next year. In the meantime, it is possible to use your phone's, or any other device's, browser to connect to the service.


What does EEG stand for?
EEG stands for electroencephalography. It is the recording of electrical activity along the scalp. This is the data fed to the Seizure Predictor and analyzed to detect if a seizure is about to occur in a patient.
